Bite Categories

Category 1

  • Feeding or Touching an Animal
  • Licking of intact skin
  • Exposure to patient with signs and symptoms of rabies by sharing utensils
  • Casual contact (Talking, Visiting, and feeding suspected rabies cases)

Category 2

  • Nibbling of uncovered skin with or without bruising/ Hematoma
  • Minor or Superficial scratches/ abrasions without bleeding including those included to bleed.
  • All Category 2 exposures on the head, neck, hand, feet, and genitals are considered Category 3.

Category 3

  • Transdermal bite
    - Punctured Wounds
    - Laceration- Avulsion
    - Scratches/ abrasion with spontaneous bleeding
  • Licks on broken skin
  • Exposure to rabies patient through bite, contamination of mucous membrane (eyes, oral/nasal mucosa, genita/ anal mucous membrane) or open skin lesion with body fluids through splattering and mouth to mouth resuscitation
  • Unprotected handling of infected carcass
  • Ingested raw infected meat
  • Exposure to bats
  • All category 2 exposures on head and neck area
